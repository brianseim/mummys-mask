Like most [Osirian] cities on the banks of the [River Sphinx], Tephu is hot, dusty, and perpetually busy. Its streets are full of hawkers and peddlers, well known for their stubbornness, sham charm, and inability to hear the word no. Visitors are immersed in this world as soon as they step off the ferry—they’re offered everything from savory minced lamb, limes, olives, mint tea, and water to carpets (some of which the sellers claim to be “magic” carpets), camel hide leather goods, hookah pipes, pesh, pots and pans, and even camels and dancing girls and boys.
